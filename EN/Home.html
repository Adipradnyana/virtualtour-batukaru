Pura Luhur Batukaru

Beranda

Information

History

The word Batukaru consists of 2 words, namely Batu which means Stone or in religion itself means strength or energy source. Meanwhile, Karu which means a shape like a coconut shell. Therefore, Batukaru itself means the power of the cover (Penangkeb in Balinese), which is seen by the use of Tirta penyidakarya batukaru in the closing process of a ceremony performed by Hindus in Bali. Based on previous written records, it is known that Batukaru Temple was founded around the 12th century AD by Mpu Kuturan during the reign of the King of Bali Kuna in the Warmadewa dynasty, King Masula Masuli who adhered to the teachings of Shiva-Buddhism.

Temple Function

Pura Luhur Batukaru has several functions, among others:
  • As a place to ask for the safety and prosperity of life by carrying out ceremonies in order to get success in farming activities.
  • As a sacred place to invoke the salvation of life for the sedharma community.
  • As an appreciation and worship of Ida Bhatara Sanghyang Bayu.
  • As a sacred place for Ida Cokorde Tabanan in building a strong order of life of the nation and state.

The uniqueness of the temple

The uniqueness of Pura Luhur Batukaru includes:
  • The location of the temple is at the foot of Mount Batukaru with a height of 833 meters above sea level which makes the atmosphere of the temple cool and not infrequently this temple is shrouded in mountain mist which provides a memorable vacation experience for temple visitors.
  • It can be used as a historical tour where the temple building is an ancient artifact of historical heritage which according to lontar relics, and a number of chronicles this temple was built in the 11th century.
  • Can be used as a religious tourist spot for the Hindu community where this temple itself has a function for a holy place to invoke salvation for the sedharma community.

Prohibition of Entry

Entering the temple area is prohibited:
Women who are pregnant.
Children who have not yet lost their teeth.
Women who are menstruating.
People in mourning/death.
Visitors who do not wear Balinese traditional clothing.
